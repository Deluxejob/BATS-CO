Safe Haven Demand — stocks vs the ultimate safe hiding place

When investors get scared, they run for cover — and the cover of choice is long-duration U.S. Treasuries. Comparing how stocks are doing against how bonds are doing gives us a direct read on whether the market is risk-on or in flight-to-safety mode.

What is Safe Haven Demand?

The classic CNN Fear & Greed "safe haven" measure. We compare the recent performance of stocks (SPY, the S&P 500 ETF) to the recent performance of long-duration U.S. Treasury bonds (TLT, the iShares 20+ Year Treasury Bond ETF). If stocks are trouncing bonds, investors are hungry for risk. If bonds are winning, they're heading for the exits.

The SPY vs TLT spread

  • SPY S&P 500 ETF — the risky-asset side.
  • TLT iShares 20+ Year Treasury Bond ETF — the safe-haven side.
  • Spread 20-day return of SPY minus 20-day return of TLT. Positive = risk-on. Negative = flight to safety.

How BATS uses it

Confirmatory (like Breadth and Junk Bond Demand). Positive spread confirms risk-on sentiment. Negative spread flags flight-to-safety. Same direction convention as our other spread-based components: score = 50 + (spread × 5), clamped to 5–95. Slightly gentler slope than Breadth or Junk because stocks and bonds decouple more sharply.

What the data actually says

Three findings from the 23-year Safe Haven backtest — a clean smile pattern:

1

Deep flight to safety = buying opportunity.

When the spread pushed below −9% (bonds crushing stocks by a wide margin, investors rushing to safety), the S&P 500 was up +13.8% on average over the next 12 months, positive 81% of the time (n=485). Panic buys the bottom, historically.

Takeaway: When everyone runs to bonds, that's often the moment the stock recovery has already started.

2

Extreme risk-on also earns forward returns.

When the spread pushed above +9% (stocks demolishing bonds, extreme risk-on), forward 12-month returns averaged +13.1%, 84% positive (n=649). Trend continuation is real.

Takeaway: Broad risk-on is rarely a top signal — it's confirmation that a healthy bull market has broad support.

3

The middle is noise.

When the spread sits between −5% and +5% (roughly 80% of days), forward returns track the baseline. Safe Haven only shouts at extremes.

Takeaway: This is a "shout at extremes, whisper the rest of the time" indicator. Perfect complement to VIX and Breadth.

How to use this information

  1. Watch the flight-to-safety extreme. When the 20-day spread pushes below −9%, history says the odds strongly favor patient stock buyers over the next 6–12 months.
  2. Don't fade extreme risk-on. Just like MA200 and NAAIM, extreme confidence in equities is not a top signal on its own — strong risk-on has historically continued.
  3. Combine with Junk Bond Demand. When both Safe Haven and Junk Bond Demand flash oversold at the same time, credit and Treasury markets are BOTH screaming buy signal. That's an unusually strong setup.
  4. Middle of the range = ignore. When SPY and TLT are within a few percent of each other, this component isn't telling you anything useful. Look elsewhere.

Reminder: None of this is investment advice. Historical patterns are not guarantees. Always do your own research or talk to a financial professional.

SHORT-TERM MOMENTUM SIGNAL

When four momentum indicators agree, listen.

This gauge fires only when four of the most-watched momentum oscillators — RSI, Stochastic, MACD, and KST — all say the same thing on the S&P 500. Unlike our BATS blend (which is a 6–12 month sentiment view), this is a 1–3 month timing tool: buy signals when the four are lifting off a bottom, sell signals when they're rolling over from a top.

What the data actually says

1

Confluence is extremely rare — and that's the point.

All four indicators agreeing at once happens on only ~2.3% of trading days since 1990. Roughly 98% of the time the gauge sits at Neutral. When it finally does fire a Buy or Sell, you're seeing something that only happens a handful of times per year.

2

Buy signals work at short horizons.

After a Strong Buy or Buy signal, the S&P 500 was positive 65% of the time over the next 5 trading days with an average gain of roughly +0.9% — versus a baseline of ~58% and +0.18% for Neutral days. Over 20 days the signals still work: +1.3–1.6% avg with 61–66% hit rates. Good short-term timing tool.

3

Your instinct was right — sell-from-top is a trap.

The Weak Sell configuration (all four momentum indicators crossing down from overbought) has historically been followed by the strongest 60-day returns of any bucket: +4.5% average with an 80% positive rate. Momentum crossing down from a top does not mean the market is about to fall — it usually keeps grinding higher. This is why the signal is labeled "Weak" despite having full confluence.

Methodology

Data source: S&P 500 daily closes since 1990, from our own data/spx.csv (refreshed daily via the GitHub Actions workflow).

Signal detection window: a crossover counts as "fresh" if it occurred within the last 5 trading days. A moderate window — strict enough to still mean something, loose enough to let the four indicators line up despite normal lag.

Per-indicator rules:

  • RSI (14-day, Wilder): BUY = RSI crossed above 30 (oversold exit) or is rising while below 50. SELL = crossed below 70 or falling while above 50.
  • Stochastic (%K=14, %D=3, close-only): BUY = %K crossed above %D (crossover from below 20 = "fresh"). SELL = %K crossed below %D (from above 80 = "fresh").
  • MACD (12, 26, 9): BUY = MACD crossed above signal line (from below zero = "fresh"). SELL = crossed below signal (from above zero = "fresh").
  • KST (Pring, 10/15/20/30 ROCs): BUY = KST crossed above its 9-day signal (from below zero = "fresh"). SELL = crossed below (from above zero = "fresh").

Composite rule:

  • Strong Buy — all 4 BUY, and at least 3 are fresh crossovers from oversold.
  • Buy — all 4 BUY, but not the fresh-oversold configuration.
  • Neutral — anything less than 4-way agreement.
  • Sell — all 4 SELL, but not the fresh-overbought configuration.
  • Weak Sell — all 4 SELL, and at least 3 are fresh crossovers from overbought (labeled "weak" because history shows this specific configuration is the least reliable of the four active states, not because it has less confluence).

Stochastic note: because data/spx.csv only carries closing prices, we use close-only Stochastic — the 14-day range of closes rather than intraday highs and lows. This is a standard variant used by most retail technicians.

Reminder: None of this is investment advice. Momentum signals fail regularly, and no combination of indicators is a crystal ball. Always do your own research or talk to a financial professional.
